Overview

Portfolio management is a decision-making process that aims to maximise return through the proportional allocation of capital into identified shares. The initial share evaluation phase involves identifying shares with suitable characteristics for investment. Stock markets are dynamic systems, and share evaluation ordinarily requires expert domain knowledge.

Existing AI techniques are black-box and provide minimal decision support. INVEST is a semantic Bayesian network-based intelligent system framework but has yet to be implemented. Spatial-temporal graph neural network (ST-GNN) applications to stock market prediction have yet to be tested, for which the forecasts can proxy share evaluation.

Semantic Bayesian Networks

Semantic Bayesian networks (SBNs) are a class of Artificial Intelligence techniques that support explainability in intelligent systems. This research empirically evaluates the INVEST intelligent decision support system and several extensions for Johannesburg Stock Exchange-listed share evaluation.

Graph Neural Networks

Spatial-temporal graph neural networks are a class of deep neural network models that process graphically-encoded multivariate data exhibiting both spatial and temporal dependencies. This data forms a spatial-temporal graph that consists of a set of nodes, each with dynamic node-level temporal inputs and a predefined graph structure.
